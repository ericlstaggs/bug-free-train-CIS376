I am 46 years old. I am a father of 3 and also animal lover. I joined the national guard right out of high school where I became a fire direction control specialist. I grew up in Collinwood TN and spent my summers in the muddy waters of Clifton TN where we had river property. I Worked at auto electronics for years as a young man until I became an electrician apprentice which fed us for years. I eventually moved on and started building industrial panels for a local shop named RLC until I moved to Georgia to start writing some full stack development for CGI. I moved on to KBR and Boeing until I landed in SAIC in Huntsville. I worked on Redstone for a couple years until I took a sabbatical after the passing of my mother. I took off on sailboats and toured the Caribbean and the Florida Keys until I decided it was time to come back home. Now I am here and I am trying to make the best out of starting over late in life. Being in the Big Idea is important as I think Nick has a device that will save lives and cut costs that should be passed onto the consumer for petroleum products. This could lead to a much larger movement for industrial parts to be created by 3D printers hopefully creating less waste, a safer working environment, and maybe a little money for the pockets that help him see this vision come to fruition.

I joined the Marine Corps in 2009 and severed through 2012. In 2013 I started work at the San Joaquin County Sherrif’s department and worked as the head Evidence Custodian managing the evidence warehouse and all evidence in San Joaquin County through 2017. In 2014 I started my custom firearms business and continue to operate it sparingly, moving more into technology development rather than custom manufacturing. It was when I was developing technology to make polymer suppressors viable that I realized there is an entire new field to put this technology in. So around early 2015, I started thinking of way to implement this technology into other safety devices. Suppressors are essentially a muffler that acts as an inline deflagration arrestor. My design could greatly improve upon the safety of current models. Knowing I needed some structured help and guidance for bringing this type of device to market I decided to join in the Big Ideas competition at UNA hoping to iron out all of my weaknesses in the adjacent fields to what I’m accustomed to.
